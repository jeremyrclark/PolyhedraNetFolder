Stellation

Classical stellation extends each face plane until new cells close up—famous on the dodecahedron and icosahedron (e.g. Miller’s set), and on the Kepler–Poinsot stars. The Stellation diagram mode plots where other face planes meet one face plane (including for the rhombic dodecahedron's rhombi). Uniform pyramids mode adds a congruent pyramid on every face (height slider). In diagram mode, region clicks build a partial classical-style stellation on the reference face; full Miller cell closure can be added later.

Fold the net fully to explore stellation.
